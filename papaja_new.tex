\abstract{
Non-coding RNAs are found to perform many important biological roles we haven't anticipated before. The discovery of new RNA gene sequences far outpaces the studies on RNA structures and functions. Methods that use sequence information for aiding the RNA structure and functions are of great importance in this sequence-rich era.
In this work, we present RNAcoup, a fully automatic method that combines RNA homolog search and evolution-coupling analysis methods. We show that the RNA homolog sequence profiles generated by RNAcoup pipeline have comparable performance to RFAM family homologs. We also show that the RNAcoup pipeline displays robust performance for RNAs with unknown RFAM families. RNAcoup should be useful for improving RNA structure modelling and functional annotations.


}
